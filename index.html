About Me

I am a PhD candidate in Cyber Studies at the University of Tulsa, specializing in Security Operations Center (SOC) analysis and cybersecurity measurement frameworks. My research focuses on enhancing cybersecurity efficiency by measuring and improving the alignment between CISO objectives and SOC analyst activities.

Previously, I served as Acting Chairperson of the Computer Science Department at Harare Institute of Technology, where I led strategic curriculum development and digitization initiatives. My academic journey combines rigorous theoretical research with practical cybersecurity experience.

My mission is to advance cybersecurity resilience through systematic research methodologies that bridge the gap between executive strategy and operational security practices. I am passionate about developing measurement frameworks that enhance SOC effectiveness and organizational security posture.

Research Focus

Current PhD Research

My doctoral research investigates the strategic coherence between Chief Information Security Officers (CISOs) and Security Operations Center (SOC) analysts. Through systematic measurement frameworks, I aim to quantify and improve the alignment of cybersecurity objectives across organizational levels.

Key Research Areas
